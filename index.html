ceremony of self

questions from black femme and queer dreamers

These questions are big, continuous and daily practices. I invite you to fill out this form as a ceremony to self, to honor yourself and your imagination. These questions are compiled from black femme thinkers and makers living a “creative life of refusal,” in the words of Alexis Pauline Gumbs. I encourage you to read What Are Your Ceremonies? Surrendering to the sacred safety embedded in the present by Ayana Zaire Cotton and Lessons on Silence from Annika Hansteen Izora.

"How can you be a source of safety for yourself? As you journey through great big unknowns, you are your most sacred offering of protection. Your dreams, art, visions, look to you as a home. Embrace self-centering as a practice. Visualize what protection looks like for you, whether affirmations, meditations where the light in you becomes a loving shield around your body, or other practices. Practice what it means for your own self to be your favorite home to return to."

Lessons from Silence - Annika Hansteen-Izora

“There are layers of poesis around practice, cycles, and loops that I am trying to get under and articulate. Layers of salt, layers of soil, layers of sound, layers of soul that it builds up and breaks down only to rebuild again. It has something to do with the creative practice and simultaneously forgetting and remembering each other. It has everything to do with the lifecycle of the forest and teaching us about our ability to surrender long enough to let spirit show you the safety inside the regenerating life-force of faith. Is forgetting part of the practice? Forgetting in the face of fear only to remember in the face of another. Does the forgetting facilitate the rebirth? Must I always leave to return? What if the practice isn’t staying in remembrance, what if the practice is creating a safe space for the cycle of forgetting — maintaining the garden of longing so we never stop looking? I’m learning to surrender control, let the saprophytes of my spirit do their job, and find safety in the cycle. To practice presence is a recursive move. A practice of a practice.”

What Are Your Ceremonies? Surrendering to the sacred safety embedded in the present - Ayana Zaire Cotton
